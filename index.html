Film Permit Data Reflects Growth in Brooklyn, While Bronx Numbers Lag

Film permit data from the city demonstrates that filming for movies, TV, and commericals is concentrated in Manhattan and Brooklyn. The growth in Brooklyn is new, evident when compared to 2017 data, and more permits are also being issued in Queens. However, Bronx numbers are low, and have remained so.

By Sophie Janus

DEC 16 2021

An actor stands on a film set at a city park. A film permit is an official certificate from a municipality that gives permission for filmmaking activities to take place. Image courtesy of the Mayor's Office of Media and Entertainment.

“We saw a void here,” said Gregory Hernandez, co-founder of Bronx 48, the film initiative behind the Bronx’s annual 48-hour filmmaking challenge.
The void described by Hernandez is reflected in data from the Mayor's Office of Media and Entertainment that records where film permits are issued in the city.
Filming in 2021 was concentrated in Manhattan and Brooklyn. The growth in Brooklyn is relatively new, demonstrated when compared to the same map using 2017 data.
In comparison, the Bronx demonstrates minimal growth. In response to this information, Hernandez said that change is necessary in order for filmmakers from the Bronx to be able to succeed and tell their stories.
“Definitely financial assistance is a necessity, but I would even say beyond that is the need for people to come up here with good intentions of collaboration and mentorship.”
